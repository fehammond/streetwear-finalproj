    Why Streetwear?

    When devising a topic to explore for this final project, I wanted to choose an area I connected with to ensure continued motivation and excitement throughout the year. Being part of the streetwear community and Basement Facebook group since I was 15, it felt like the perfect area to investigate, using my existing knowledge to my advantage.

    I love (almost) everything about streetwear. The supportiveness of the community, the endless sub-styles within it and the opportunities that arise from being in a group of creatives.

    One thing I don’t love about streetwear is the use of purchasing bots snapping up items in seconds, meaning myself and others can’t purchase what we want for retail. I probably won’t ever use a bot, and I didn’t initially completely understand them. This is what drove me to dig deeper into the topic.

    Skills and Education

    Throughout my three years of university I have developed and improved a variety of creative digital skills including web design and development, creative asset production and social media content creation.

    My passion lies within front-end development and I’m constantly working towards a skillset that will help me enter web dev without a sweat. I have a strong foundation in HTML and CSS and am currently learning JavaScript.

    Alongside my studies, I've grafted! I've been a social media account manager and content creator within an agency, working with an extremely varied set of clients. Here I’ve exercised skills learned at university, but also developed others such as time-management and client interaction.

    I have invaluable work experience within the creative industry, particularly as an assistant on photoshoots with the likes of Burberry, Mario Valentino and Ellesse. Working with the big dogs such as Farfetch, Spring Studios and Focus Brands threw me in at the deep end and ever since, I’ve aspired to think big.
